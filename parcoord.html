Discussion

In this parallel coordinates chart I wanted to show what percentage of upward mobility children came from parents in the 5 quintile, what tier of college they went to, and finally the median income for the kids.
The paper makes this statement Rates of bottom-to-top quintile mobility are highest at certain mid-tier public universities, such as the City University of New York and California State colleges. and despite not showing the names of the universities, it's shown that a fair distribution of the upward mobility kids do in fact go to a mid tier college. There is also a fair amount of upward mobility for kids who went to a two year college (tier 9).
Finally it seems more likely that these kids whil have a higher median salary if they attended a four year mid tier college(tier 5, tier 6).
